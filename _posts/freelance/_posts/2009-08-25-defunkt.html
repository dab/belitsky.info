Hello. I have 9 questions to you about working process and programming. So, when you'll be able - please answer them. Or just some of them. I want become Ruby Pro and earning money with fun.

Great!

How you find your first Ruby related job?

I was doing PHP consulting and convinced some of my clients to try out Ruby on Rails. They agreed, I gave them a bit of a discount, and everyone was happy.

Where and how do you search for work now? Can you give me some advice on the best ways to find some Ruby related work?

I work full time at GitHub. Start your own company - that's a great way to find work ;)

What advice would you give a Ruby beginner without any projects ready to show?

Open source! Write and release projects, fix bugs in other peoples' projects, be active in the community. The best way to improve is by writing and reading code.

What you want tell to past yourself from present moment about working with Ruby, clients, how to find good clients etc.? You know, many people dreaming about changing past for better present moment, so anything you want to change in past yourself, write about it.

Don't give up and don't worry. Work hard and stay focused, but don't work too hard. Be patient. Working hard is definitely the secret to success.

What books or sites or recipes or whatever else you can recommend (it may be about productivity or negotiation or thinking - anything you think will help me live better life as programmer)?

I love rubyinside.com for staying on top of the latest Ruby news. I also follow many popular Rubyists like technoweenie and nzkoz on Twitter to stay in the loop.

I think the secret to being a better programmer is to stop caring what other people think of you, stop worrying about appearances and judgement, and instead just start programming. It's not easy but it's always the best tactic.

How much time a week you work and how you keep yourself productive and focused?

I'm not sure. I start work between 8:30 and 10 most days, then work until 6 or 7 if I'm going out that night. If I'm not going out I'll work until 1 or 2am. I don't really worry about how many hours I work - instead I focus on what I need to get done.

I find planning what you will do tomorrow is the best way to stay productive. That way it's easy to get up and immediately start working - no need to spend time figuring out what to do or where to start. Think about what you'll do tomorrow tonight.

How you organize your workspace and what tools you using while working?

I don't really have a workspace. I work from home and constantly move between laying in bed, working on my couch, working at my office desk, and working at the dinner table. I like to move around.

Emacs and Git are the most important tools in my toolbox.

How to be successful and *profitable* programmer?

Like I said - work hard and write a lot of code. Don't get hung up on appearances or what other people think. Don't worry about reading about design patterns or methodologies - learn by doing.

What every programmer should know?

Take breaks throughout the day. I like playing guitar and Rock Band.
